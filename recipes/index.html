Mexican

Chicken Enchiladas

Ingredients

-          1 can cream of chicken soup

-          ½ cup sour cream

-          1 cup salsa

-          2 tsp chili powder

-          2 cups cooked chicken

-          ½ cup Monterey jack cheese

-          6 flour tortillas

-          1 small tomato

-          1 green onion(scallion)

Directions

  1. Combine soup, sour cream, salsa, and chili powder. 
  2. Stir 1 cup of salsa mix with chicken and cheese. 
  3. Divide chicken mixture among tortillas. 
  4. Roll them up and place seam-side-down in a dish. 
  5. Pour remaining salsa mixture over tortillas. 
  6. Cover and bake at 350 for 30-40 minutes (until bubbly)
  7. Top with tomatoes and scallion

Best Cookies Ever

Ingredients

-          1 ¾ cups (8.75 oz) all-purpose flour (Store brand is fine, but King Arthur is superior)

-          ½ teaspoon baking soda

-          14 tablespoons (1 ¾ sticks) salted butter (Store brand is fine, but higher fat % is superior)

-          ¾ cup + a little extra (5.75 oz) dark brown sugar (Store brand is fine, but organic/unrefined is superior)

-          ½ cup (3.5 oz) sugar

-          1 teaspoon salt

-          2 teaspoons vanilla extract

-          1 egg

-          1 egg yolk

-          Chocolate chips or chopped baking chocolate to your preference

-          A scale for measuring out weights (recommended to get the best texture)

Directions

  1. Preheat oven to 375 degrees, but whenever you want since it might take a while especially if you chill it.
  2. Whisk flour and baking soda in a medium bowl and set aside.
  3. Mix sugars and salt in large mixing bowl. I like breaking down the brown sugar so it’s all granular with the others.
  4. Put 10 tablespoons (1 ¼ sticks) butter in a saucepan (don’t use black pans because its hard to tell when browned) and heat on medium-high heat, stirring constantly until browned (like 3 minutes). For extra caramelly/nutty flavor, wait 5-7 seconds after you think it’s done. Also, some people use a pot for this so if you want to do that go right ahead.
  5. Transfer into small, heatproof bowl. Add the remaining 4 tablespoons (½ stick) of butter and let it melt together.
  6. Add butter, vanilla, and eggs to sugars/salt and mix until fully incorporated. Wait 3 minutes, then mix it again for 30 seconds. And then do that again. And then one more time. It should be smooth and light brown and bubbly.
  7. Add the flour and mix. Then chocolate-chip-ify it to your heart’s content.
  8. Divide the dough up however you want. It usually breaks down into 15 cookies + a few spoonful’s of dough.
  9. Cook it for 9-11 (never forget) minutes, checking frantically. You could chill it, but I’ve never done that and don’t know how it affects cooking times. I think they usually say to chill for at least 30 minutes.

 

Cinnamon Rolls

Ingredients

DOUGH

-          ¼ oz yeast package

-          ½ cup milk (scalded)

-          ½ cup warm water

-          ¼ cup sugar

-          1/3 cup butter (softened)

-          1 teaspoon salt

-          1 egg

-          3.5 cups flour

FILLING

-          ½ cup butter (melted)

-          ¾ cup sugar

-          2 tablespoons cinnamon

Directions

  1. Warm milk in microwave until bubbling (I’m not sure what constitutes scalded for nonfat milk)
  2. Dissolve yeast in warm water; stir a bit to incorporate
  3. Mix milk, butter, sugar, salt, and egg in bowl of stand mixer
  4. Add 2 cups of flour, mix. Then add yeast mixture, mix. Then add remaining flour and mix.
  5. Knead dough on medium speed (setting 4) for 6-8 minutes
  6. Let rise for ~2 hours in a clean, oiled bowl (covered with plastic wrap and a towel) until doubled in size
  7. Heat oven to 350; roll out dough into large rectangle and spread filling mixture on it
  8. Roll up the dough and cut into equal pieces.
  9. Line in pan and bake for 30 minutes. Top with regular frosting recipe.

Italian

Roman Style Chicken

Ingredients

-          4 skinless chicken breast halves, with ribs

-          2 skinless chicken thighs, with bones

-          1/2 teaspoon salt, plus 1 teaspoon

-          1/2 teaspoon freshly ground black pepper, plus 1 teaspoon

-          1/4 cup olive oil

-          1 red bell pepper, sliced

-          1 yellow bell pepper, sliced

-          3 ounces prosciutto, chopped

-          2 cloves garlic, chopped

-          1 (15-ounce) can diced tomatoes

-          1/2 cup white wine

-          1 tablespoon fresh thyme leaves

-          1 teaspoon fresh oregano leaves

-          1/2 cup chicken stock

-          2 tablespoons capers

-          1/4 cup chopped fresh flat-leaf parsley leaves

Directions

Season the chicken with 1/2 teaspoon salt and 1/2 teaspoon pepper. In a heavy, large skillet, heat the olive oil over medium heat. When the oil is hot, cook the chicken until browned on both sides. Remove from the pan and set aside.

Keeping the same pan over medium heat, add the peppers and prosciutto and cook until the peppers have browned and the prosciutto is crisp, about 5 minutes. Add garlic and cook for 1 minute. Add tomatoes, wine, and herbs. Using a wooden spoon, scrape the browned bits off the bottom of the pan. Return chicken to the pan, add the stock, and bring the mixture to a boil. Reduce heat and simmer, covered, until the chicken is cooked through, about 20 to 30 minutes.

If serving immediately, add the capers and the parsley. Stir to combine and serve. If making ahead of time, transfer the chicken and sauce to a storage container, cool, and refrigerate. The next day, reheat the chicken to a simmer over medium heat. Stir in the capers and the parsley and serve.

Chicken Pot Pie

Crust

-          1 1/4 cups flour

-          1/2 teaspoon salt

-          10 tablespoons unsalted butter

-          1 egg yolk

-          3 tablespoons ice water

 

  1. Add flour and salt to food processor and pulse once or twice.
  2. Add all butter at once, and process for 10 seconds (be careful not to overprocess). It’ll look like beads of butter encapsulated flour.
  3. Add egg yolk and process for a few more seconds (it’s ok if it’s not completely incorporated.
  4. Pour into a bowl and add the ice water, and form into a ball. If it’s too wet you can add more flour.
  5. Wrap in plastic/beeswrap and freeze for 10 minutes.

 

 

Pie

-          3-3.5 lbs chicken breast (or thigh or whatever)

-          2-3 carrots (carrots are very subjective in size and amount so use your judgment)

-          Some pearl onions (optional, but add a subtle oniony flavor I’d imagine)

-          3 bay leaves

-          4 cups chicken stock 

-          2 tablespoons unsalted butter 

-          2 tablespoons flour

-          1/2 cup heavy cream

-          1 cup peas

 

 

  1. Preheat the oven to 375 degrees.
  2. Chop up ya veggies.
  3. Season the chicken with like a good amount of salt and pepper and cook fully.
  4. While the chicken is cooking, boil the chicken stock with the bay leaves in a large pot until the chicken stock has been reduced by about half. Take off heat and set aside.
  5. While the chicken stock is boiling, melt 2 tablespoons of butter over medium heat in a deep pan or skillet. When the butter is melted, add all vegetables except the peas. Stir frequently until the vegetables are softened (5-ish minutes).
  6. When the vegetables are softened, add the 2 tablespoons of flour and stir until just tan.
  7. Pour the chicken stock and heavy cream into the vegetable-flour mixture and stir constantly until the desired thickness is achieved, cooking over medium-low to medium heat (you want the mixture bubbling but not boiling).
  8. Chop or pull apart the chicken until you have like 3 cups (but again, it’s your pie so load it up how you want). Add chicken and peas to the pan and mix it all up.
  9. Pour mixture into pie pan, and roll out the dough to the desired thickness and cover the pie pan, trimming excess and doing whatever the hell you want with it. Cut slits into the pie crust all fancy-like to release steam, and do fancy stuff to the edge (I just press a fork into it).
  10. Beat an egg (not my cat) and brush over the pie crust. Cook for 50ish minutes or until the crust is deeply golden brown and the filling is bubbling.
  11. Don’t even let it sit, get in there and eat cause it’s so good.

Clam Chowder

Ingredients

-          7 lb cherrystone clams – scrubbed and rinsed

-          3 cups water

-          4 bacon strips

-          1 med spanish onion, small dice

-          2 tbs flour

-          3 large red pot – ½ inch dice

-          1 tsp fresh thyme

-          2 tbs fresh parsley

-          1 cup heavy cream

Directions

Add clams to 3 cups of water.  Boil until cooked (just open) about 10 min.  remove clams.  Set aside.  Discard any clams that do not open.  Strain broth in a sieve lined with coffee filter and then set aside.  Clean pot.  Over med heat brown bacon.  Discard all but 2 tbl bacon fat.  Cook onion in fat.  Stir in flour.  Cook for 1 minute.  Do not brown flour.  Wisk in clam broth.  Add potatoes and thyme.  Simmer 10 minutes.  Remove clams from shells, reserve any liquid, chop the clams.  Strain the liquid then add it with clams to pot.  Stir in parsley and cream. Cook for about 3 minutes (until clams are heated through).

Pretzels

Ingredients

-          1 1/2 cups warm (110 to 115 degrees F) water

-          1 tablespoon sugar

-          2 teaspoons salt

-          1 package active dry yeast

-          22 ounces all-purpose flour, approximately 4 1/2 cups

-          2 ounces butter, melted

-          Vegetable oil, for bowl

-          10 cups water

-          2/3 cup baking soda

-          1 large egg yolk beaten with 1 tablespoon water

-          Pretzel salt

Directions

  1. Combine water, sugar, salt, and yeast in bowl of stand mixer. Allow to sit until it starts foaming/bubbling
  2. Add flour and butter, and mix on low until combined.
  3. Change to medium speed (setting 4) and knead until the down is smooth and pulls away from the side of the bowl, approximately 4 to 5 minutes.
  4. Let rise for 1-2 hours in a clean, oiled bowl (covered with plastic wrap and a towel) until doubled in size
  5. Heat oven to 450 degrees
  6. Bring 10 cups water and baking soda to rolling boil in pot
  7. Take dough and divide into balls, and place 1 by 1 (or in small groups) into boiling water for 30 seconds
  8. Remove them from the water and place them on paper towels to dry out. Sprinkle with coarse sea salt or pretzel salt.
  9. Transfer to baking sheet and brush with egg wash. Bake for 12-14 minutes or until dark golden brown.

Queso

Ingredients

-          8 oz. American Cheese (preferably in one large chunk from the deli, but deli people usually freak out when you request a big hunk of cheese – you can get slices but it’s just harder to grate/reduce into little pieces)

-          8 oz. Monterey Jack Cheese

-          2 tablespoons unsalted butter

-          3 garlic cloves (or more depending on how garlic-crazy you are)

-          1 teaspoon kosher salt

-          2 teaspoons ground cumin

-          ¼ teaspoon cayenne pepper (or to taste)

-          2 cups milk

-          1 tablespoon cornstarch

Directions

  1. Grate all the cheese – if you couldn’t convince the deli to give you a half pound hunk of American cheese and you’re working with slices, it’s okay to rip them into larger shreds. Melting will be a bit slower but American cheese won’t separate if it’s under heat for a bit longer.
  2. Press or mince or grate the garlic – doesn’t really matter as long as they’re teeny tiny pieces so the queso isn’t chunky.
  3. Prep the milk: take ¼ cup out of the 2 cups of milk and add the cornstarch to it. Stir the cornstarch in well and ensure that there are no clumps or chunks (we want the cornstarch hydrated so it doesn’t make the queso chalky and gross).
  4. In a large saucepan, heat the butter over medium heat until it’s a bit foamy.
  5. Throw in the garlic and cook ‘em until they’re juuuuuust starting to brown.
  6. Throw in the salt, cumin, and cayenne and swish it around in the butter until its aromatic.
  7. Pour in 1 ¼ cups of milk and let come to a simmer. Once it’s simmering, get your elbows ready for some stirring. Save the remaining ½ for fine tuning the texture (you don’t want a runny queso). Stir at a simmer for a couple minutes.
  8. Pour in the milk/cornstarch mixture and let come back to a simmer, and then keep stirring until the mixture doesn’t drip off your stirring implement.
  9. Turn the heat down to medium-low. Begin adding the American cheese one handful at a time and stirring it vigorously until its all melted together. Then the next handful. And the next. And then do the same with the Monterey Jack. You should always be stirring because we don’t want this cheese to simmer because it will break up the cheese.
  10. Add milk until the texture is good enough to coat a chip when you dip in normally.

Katie’s Dank Bread

Ingredients

-          500 g bread flour

-          375ish g water

-          50 g sourdough starter

-          13 g salt

Directions

Mix flour and water until flour is hydrated; let sit for one hour (this is the autolyse). Add sourdough starter, mix, and rest for 30 minutes. Mix in salt; let rest for 30 minutes. Perform three sets of stretch and folds spread out over the next few hours. Then, let rise until jiggly with a few bubbles on the surface. The rise won't be dramatic. For me, this can take several hours since our apartment is cold. Pre-shape, scooting the ball of dough around on the counter to increase surface tension. Cover with bowl upside-down and let rest 20 minutes. Shape into a round or log, put into a banneton or a towel-lined bowl, and put in the fridge overnight. (I usually start the bread in the morning and shape around dinner time.)

The next day (at any point), preheat the oven to 500° with a covered Dutch oven inside. When preheated, turn dough out of banneton and score the top. Slide into the Dutch oven and cook, covered, for 19 minutes. Remove bread from Dutch oven and put directly on oven rack, turn down the heat to 400º, and cook until browned to your liking, usually 15–25 minutes. Let cool before slicing.
